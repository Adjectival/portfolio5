About Me

After years of dabbling with building simple websites, I took the plunge into a career as a developer by enrolling in an intensive 6 month course in CSS/JS/Design at Epicodus. In their Frontend Dev track I learned to prototype, to design, and then to build and style web applications. While interning at a small Portland startup last year, I used Bootstrap 4 to make their existing application platform more responsive and easier to use.

For a few years in the military I worked to maintain and sometimes deploy data and voice networks and I even had some experience with legacy stuff that was highly classified. I loved the variety of technologies I learned to operate and troubleshoot. Recently I worked as a validation engineer on contract at Intel, which taught me much about the value of automation of tests on pre-market platforms.

After moving to Oregon in 2011 I earned a Bachelor's of Science in Psychology with my military GIBill. I try to channel what I learned about perception and neuroscience as an undergrad when wireframing and designing user interfaces.

Last fall my wife and I finally took our honeymoon, in Seoul!
