About Me

After years of dabbling with building simple websites, I took the plunge into a career as a developer by enrolling in an intensive 6 month course in CSS/JS/Design at Epicodus. In their Front End track I learned to prototype, to design, and then to build and style web applications. While interning at a small start-up recently, I used Bootstrap 4 to make their existing application platform more responsive and easier to use. I've realized that I like using technology when it influences and affects other people, and also that I can learn it much more easily when working with others.

For a few years in the military I worked to maintain and sometimes deploy data and voice networks and I even had some experience with legacy stuff that was highly classified. After a childhood spent messing around with Windows, this work seemed like a natural enough next step and I loved the variety of technologies I was trained to operate and troubleshoot.

I left to finish my bachelor's degree using the GIBill. I built simple websites for friends and dabbled in affiliate marketing and AdWords. Exploring majors lead me to Psychology. My lifelong love of technology and user interfaces steered me into code school in 2016 so I can work on crafting and improving UI/UX directly.
